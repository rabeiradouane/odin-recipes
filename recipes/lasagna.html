Classic Beef & Ricotta Lasagna

This version uses the traditional American-Italian method of layering a rich meat sauce with a creamy ricotta cheese filling. It’s a hearty, "crowd-pleaser" style dish that results in thick, cheesy slices.

Short Description: A comforting, multi-layered masterpiece featuring a savory beef ragù, a three-cheese ricotta blend, and perfectly melted mozzarella. It’s the ultimate Sunday dinner classic that tastes even better as leftovers.

Recipe (Serves 8-10):

  • Ingredients:
    • Pasta: 12–15 Lasagna noodles.
    • Meat Sauce: 1 lb Ground beef, 1 diced onion, 2 cloves garlic, 1 jar (approx. 24 oz) marinara sauce, and 1 tsp Italian seasoning.
    • Cheese Filling: 15 oz Ricotta cheese, 1 egg (beaten), ½ cup grated Parmesan, and 2 tbsp fresh parsley.
    • Topping: 3 cups shredded Mozzarella cheese.
  • Instructions:
    • Prep Sauce: Brown the beef with onions and garlic in a large skillet. Drain excess fat, stir in the marinara sauce and seasonings, and simmer for 10–15 minutes.
    • Mix Cheeses: In a bowl, combine the ricotta, egg, Parmesan, and parsley until smooth.
    • Boil Pasta: Cook noodles in salted boiling water until al dente (usually 1–2 minutes less than the box suggests), then drain.
    • Assemble: In a 9x13-inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce. Add a layer of noodles, followed by a third of the ricotta mixture, a third of the meat sauce, and a sprinkle of mozzarella. Repeat layers twice more.
    • Bake: Top with the remaining mozzarella. Cover with foil and bake at 190°C (375°F) for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for another 20–25 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden.
    • Rest: Let it sit for at least 15 minutes before cutting to ensure the layers stay together.
